Lodgify vs. Hostaway vs. hiring a studio: which is right for your estate?

A financially grounded look at three paths to a direct booking website—and a simple framework for deciding which one fits.

At some point, most estate owners running $3,000-to-$8,000-per-night properties arrive at the same decision point. The platform fees are no longer abstract. The math on guest data has started to sting. The idea of a direct booking website for vacation rental guests makes obvious financial sense. What is less obvious is how to get there—and whether a SaaS vacation rental website builder, a property management system with website features, or a bespoke studio is the right tool for a property at this tier.

The honest answer is that it depends on where you are in the business. Here is what each path actually gives you.

Lodgify

Lodgify is a vacation rental website builder aimed primarily at independent hosts and small portfolio operators. It offers templated site designs, a built-in booking engine, channel sync with Airbnb and Vrbo, and payment processing—all for a monthly subscription that starts around $17 and tops out below $100 at most tiers. For a host with one or two properties generating under $200,000 annually, that value proposition is real. You get a functional website, a channel manager, and direct booking capability without hiring anyone.

Where the Lodgify website builder runs into friction is at the luxury tier. The templates are designed for volume and accessibility, not brand differentiation. Every site built on the platform shares infrastructure, shares font choices, shares the underlying booking flow. An estate charging $5,500 per night in Aspen is a fundamentally different product from a $400-per-night mountain cabin—but nothing about a Lodgify build communicates that distinction. Guests at this price point are comparing your property to private resorts and boutique hotels, not to other vacation rentals. A shared-template website does not clear that bar.

Hostaway

Hostaway is a property management system first. The Hostaway website builder is one feature inside a broader platform built for operators managing dozens or hundreds of properties—handling reservations, unified inboxes, housekeeping tasks, reporting, and multi-channel sync. If you are operating a portfolio at scale, that infrastructure is genuinely valuable. The website capability comes along with the rest of the system, and for operators who need the operations tooling more than the brand work, that bundling makes sense.

For a single ultra-luxury estate, the tradeoff is the same one you face with Lodgify, but compounded. You are paying for substantial operational tooling you may not need, and the website output is still template-driven and platform-hosted. Your domain lives on Hostaway’s infrastructure. The booking engine looks like every other Hostaway property. The guest-facing experience is efficient, but efficiency is not the signal a $6,000-per-night estate needs to send. At this tier, the website is not a utility—it is the first material expression of what a guest is about to pay for.

Hiring a studio

A bespoke build is a different category of investment entirely, and it is worth being precise about what that means financially. A custom direct booking website for vacation rental guests—built to spec, on your own domain, with your own booking infrastructure—typically runs between $8,000 and $25,000 depending on scope. You own the code. You own the design. You own the guest data that flows through it. There are no ongoing platform fees beyond whatever payment processing and hosting you choose, both of which are commodity costs.

What you are buying is not a website. You are buying brand fidelity—a guest experience that is indistinguishable in quality from the property it represents. You are buying guest ownership—every inquiry, every email address, every returning guest belongs to you, not to a platform. And you are buying permanence. The asset compounds. A guest who books directly once, receives a personalized pre-arrival experience, and gets a direct outreach the following season is worth multiple times what a platform booking produces—not because the nightly rate is different, but because the relationship is.

The break-even math is not complicated. On a $600,000-revenue estate paying 15.5% in platform fees, converting even 15% of bookings to direct recovers the studio investment in a single season. Most estates that make this transition find that direct bookings grow steadily year over year as the guest list compounds.

How to decide

The right tool depends on two variables: your current revenue and your goals for the guest relationship. If you are generating under $150,000 annually and still building your booking history, a platform like Lodgify gives you functional direct booking capability at low cost—the brand limitations are real, but so is the budget constraint. If you are operating a multi-property portfolio and need unified operations tooling above all else, Hostaway’s system solves a genuine problem even if the website output is secondary.

If you are running a single estate above $400,000 in annual revenue, have a returning guest base, and are paying meaningful platform fees on bookings that would have come back without the platform’s help—a custom build is the financially sound decision. The question is not whether you can afford it. The question is how many more seasons you can afford not to have it.
